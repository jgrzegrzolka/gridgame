Privacy

Yet Another Quiz is a free hobby site. We collect the minimum data needed to make features work and nothing else — no tracking, no ads, no accounts.

What we store

Device ID
A random identifier generated by your browser the first time you trigger a feature that needs it (submitting a daily puzzle, saving a quiz personal best, joining an online tic-tac-toe room, setting a nickname). It lives in your browser's localStorage as gridgame.deviceId and on our server alongside any results you save. Not linked to your name, email, or any real-world identity.
Nickname (optional)
Only if you set one on the profile page. Shown publicly on the daily leaderboard if you score well that day and in online tic-tac-toe rooms alongside your role.
Game results
Your daily-puzzle submissions, flag-quiz personal bests + play counts, and online tic-tac-toe outcomes per opponent. Linked to your device ID, not to you.

Where it lives

Azure Cosmos DB in West Europe (Microsoft's Amsterdam region). Static HTML and JavaScript are served from Cloudflare's global CDN, which sees standard HTTP request metadata (IP, user agent) for routing but doesn't receive your device ID or scores.

How long we keep it

  • Daily leaderboard rows: 48 hours, then automatically purged.
  • Daily-puzzle submissions: 1 year, then automatically purged.
  • Profile, quiz personal bests, tic-tac-toe history: kept until you delete them.

What we don't do

  • No tracking, no analytics, no advertising networks, no browser fingerprinting.
  • No third-party sharing. Your data stays on our infrastructure.
  • No emails, no accounts, no passwords — the device ID is the only identifier.

Your rights

Delete everything: visit the profile page and use the Delete button (coming soon — until it ships, email the address at the bottom and your data will be purged within a few days).

Clearing your browser's storage for this domain wipes your device ID locally; server-side rows stay until deleted via the above method.

Cookies

We don't use cookies. The site stores a couple of preferences in your browser's localStorage (your device ID, language pick, nickname cache, last-played quiz variant) so the site remembers what you're doing between visits. None of it is sent to any third party.
